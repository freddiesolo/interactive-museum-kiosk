Igbo Cuisine ‚Äì Vegetable Soup & Swallow
Example from south-east Nigeria

Igbo meals often balance soups made with leafy greens, fish or assorted meat and a starchy ‚Äúswallow‚Äù such as pounded yam, fufu or semolina. Families gather around a shared bowl and eat with washed hands, making the meal a very social moment.

This sample dish shows a spicy vegetable soup served with soft swallow ‚Äì warming, filling and perfect after exploring the museum.

Yoruba Cuisine ‚Äì Efo Riro
Spinach stew from south-west Nigeria

Efo riro is a rich vegetable stew cooked with spinach or ugu leaves, tomatoes, peppers and onions, often with beef, tripe or fish. It is usually served alongside amala, eba or rice and is loved for its bright colour and layered flavours.

On our kiosk, this dish represents the colourful, peppery side of Yoruba cooking that fills streets and homes with amazing aromas.

Hausa Cuisine ‚Äì Masa & Stew
Fermented rice cakes from northern Nigeria

Masa are soft, slightly sour rice cakes made from fermented batter and cooked in special moulds. They are fluffy on the inside and lightly crisp on the outside, usually eaten with spicy tomato or pepper stew.

This dish highlights the Hausa love for grains and fermented flavours ‚Äì perfect street food to enjoy with tea or grilled meat.
