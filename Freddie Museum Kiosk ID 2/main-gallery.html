Bronze Head of an Ife Ruler
Copper alloy • Yoruba, Ile-Ife • c. 14th–15th century

This royal portrait head shows the refined metal-casting tradition of Ile-Ife in south-west Nigeria. The detailed crown and facial markings reflect status, spirituality and artistry in Yoruba court life.

Beaded Festival Mask
Wood, pigment, beads, cowrie shells • West Africa • 20th century

Masks like this appear in masquerades that combine music, dance and storytelling. The bold geometric patterns and beadwork celebrate identity, protection and community memory.

Indigo-Dyed Adire Cloth
Cotton, indigo dye • Yoruba, south-west Nigeria • 20th century

Adire cloths are hand-dyed textiles created using resist techniques such as tying, stitching or starch. Each pattern carries names and stories, turning everyday clothing into a moving gallery of symbols.
